Textbook Reviews

At the risk of making an empty point, there are a lot of textbooks out there. Some are bad, most will get you what you need, and a select few are really important. This last category populated by what are termed "canonical" texts: they're the ones everyone who wants to attain mastery of the subject should read.

Here's a list of some of the texts I have read. I'll make notes of the ones that are important texts in their fields.

Classical Mechanics

Classical Dynamics of Particles and Systems, J. Marion & S. Thornton

This textbook is really junk. Unfortunately, it's found itself a little niche in the physics curriculum because it's one of the few (and easily the most popular) book written for the level of study you'll probably be at by the time you take your first "real" (non-introductory level) courses on classical mechanics. At Waterloo, the AMATH department uses Taylor's Classical Mechanics, which I hear (but have not confirmed) is a superior text.

Don't get me wrong, M&T has everything you need per se, but it gets lost in itself with clumsy formalism. It attempts to be a more mathematical view of classical mechanics without the elegance it could avail itself of in so-doing. If at all possible, steer clear.
