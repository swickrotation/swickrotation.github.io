Once More Unto the Breach

Tomorrow marks the first day of one of my final semesters at the University of Waterloo, at the very least as an undergraduate student. This semester is mostly patching holes in coursework I should have probably officially taken by now, so I'll use the coinciding downtime to do more thinking about what I want to do post-degree. Presently I only have vague notions of wanting to do work in RQI and QG, but I'm not well-acquainted enough to know exactly where to start. For now, learning as much math as I can seems like a good option. A friend of mine handed me this paper, which implies that if gravitation can only be moddled classically, that gravity cannot create entanglement between gravitationally coupled resonators. To me, this would imply that if you can show (experimentally?) that you can entangle systems with gravity, that a model descibing such an interaction well would be a pretty good start for a theory of quantum gravity. However, the paper relies on a lot of math I don't know yet, particularly in stochastics, so I didn't follow into super-rigorous detail.
