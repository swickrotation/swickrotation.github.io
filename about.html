Bio-Lite/Academic Interests

Hey there, thanks for checking out my site. My name's Will, I'm a student of mathematical physics in the faculty of science at the University of Waterloo. I was born in downtown Toronto, and raised in Fredericton, New Brunswick. My primary academic interests are in cosmology, quantum information, and quantum gravity, though I dabble in pure math, cryptography, and network security. I've worked as a researcher in optical physics for both public and private organizations. I like to volunteer when I can, too!

What else am I into?

In art, I'm partial to Modigliani, Picasso, Edvard Munch, Alfonse Mucha, M.C. Escher, Marc Chagall, and Henri Toulouse-Lautrec.

I used to play drums in a marching band, but never took to it with any particular talent. I aspire to learn at least one instrument to the level of competent amateur, leaning towards piano at the moment, but thinking violin occasionally. I appreciate the music of Miles Davis, Radiohead, Amy Winehouse, Bob Dylan, Beethoven, Liszt, Hey Rosetta!, Margaret Glaspy, Elvis Costello, Frank Ocean, Bon Iver, Black Flag, and the Dead Kennedys to name a few. While working, I mostly listen to classical music, jazz, or deep house music (particularly while programming).

I also try to read a lot. Good books have always really helped me out of ruts. I've found literature keeps the brain quick and the tongue sharp. There are too many authors to whom I owe thanks to list, and many more on top of that I've planned to read but haven't managed yet. I'll simply name a few I really like here, but there are more and certainly people who deserve to be here who I've rather flippantly omitted. A subset of the favourites are Allen Ginsberg, James Baldwin, Virginia Woolf, Albert Camus, and Franz Kafka. I owe special mention to Ludwik Fleck- whose book greatly shifted my mentality with regards to scientific epistemology.

If I were a Springer-Verlag Graduate Text in Mathematics, I would be Frank Warner's Foundations of Differentiable Manifolds and Lie Groups.

I give a clear, detailed, and careful development of the basic facts on manifold theory and Lie Groups. I include differentiable manifolds, tensors and differentiable forms. Lie groups and homogenous spaces, integration on manifolds, and in addition provide a proof of the de Rham theorem via sheaf cohomology theory, and develop the local theory of elliptic operators culminating in a proof of the Hodge theorem. Those interested in any of the diverse areas of mathematics requiring the notion of a differentiable manifold will find me extremely useful.
